Understanding Medicare

  1. Medicare Overview

    Medicare is a federal health insurance program primarily for:

    • People aged 65 and older
    • Younger individuals with certain disabilities

    While Medicare covers many healthcare costs, it doesnâ€™t cover everything. Here's a quick overview of its parts:

  2. Medicare Parts
    • Part A (Hospital Insurance): Covers inpatient hospital stays, care in a skilled nursing facility, hospice care, and some home health care.
    • Part B (Medical Insurance): Covers certain doctors' services, outpatient care, medical supplies, and preventive services.
    • Part D (Prescription Drug Coverage): Helps cover the cost of prescription drugs.
  3. Why Consider Medicare Advantage and Supplement Plans?
    • Medicare Advantage Plans (Part C)
      • Comprehensive Coverage: Medicare Advantage plans offer an alternative to Original Medicare. They often include Part A, Part B, and sometimes Part D, all bundled together. Many plans offer additional benefits like vision, dental, and hearing coverage.
      • Cost-Effective: Often include lower premiums and out-of-pocket costs compared to separate policies.
      • Network-Based: Typically use provider networks to deliver care, which can provide more coordinated services.
      • Wellness Programs: Many plans offer wellness programs and benefits that promote healthy living.
    • Medicare Supplement Plans (Medigap)
      • Fills Coverage Gaps: Medigap plans are designed to cover out-of-pocket costs not covered by Original Medicare, such as copayments, coinsurance, and deductibles.
      • Flexibility: Allows you to see any doctor or specialist that accepts Medicare, without the need for referrals.
      • Financial Predictability: Provides more predictable healthcare costs, making it easier to budget for medical expenses.
